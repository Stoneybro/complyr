## Reviewer Access Model

Complyr supports external reviewers through `AuditRegistry` and the auditor portal.

The contract defines three reviewer access levels:

- `None`
- `Signal`
- `Full`

## What Each Level Means

### Signal

Signal access allows a reviewer to:

- create private review tests;
- receive encrypted review results;
- decrypt flagged outcomes they are permitted to read.

This is the minimum access level required for the findings workflow.

### Full

Full access includes everything in Signal, plus:

- encrypted ledger decryption rights for stored records;
- access to report rollups already granted through the contract;
- richer visibility in the auditor portal.

## Important Constraint: Access Revocation

One trust-model detail deserves explicit documentation:

removing an auditor from the active list does not retroactively revoke every ciphertext permission that was already granted at the FHE layer.

The contract itself documents this in `removeAuditor()`:

- the UI access is removed;
- future grants stop;
- historical ciphertext permissions may still exist.

## Why Reviewers Matter To The Hackathon Story

The reviewer model is where Complyr moves beyond encrypted storage into encrypted computation with scoped disclosure.

That is a stronger use of Zama technology than simply hiding fields at rest.
